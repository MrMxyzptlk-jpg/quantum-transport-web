Problem 3 — DQD with Decoherence in an Aharonov-Bohm Circuit

Interactive model for DQD with decoherence in an Aharonov-Bohm circuit

Problem Assignment

Extend the Double Quantum Dot model to include decoherence within the D'Amato-Pastawski (non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian) framework.

Consider an Aharonov-Bohm interferometer where orbitals A and B are independently coupled to environmental reservoirs (analogous to voltage probes).

You may assume:

  • \( V_{AB} = V_U = 1 \)
  • \( \varepsilon_A = \varepsilon_B = \varepsilon_C = 0\)
  1. Analyze analytically the effect of decoherence on resonances and antiresonances from analitical derivations and numerical plots.
Theoretical Derivation - Decoherence in the Aharonov-Bohm DQD

1. Theoretical Derivations

We extend the coherent Double Quantum Dot Aharonov-Bohm circuit by including independent decoherence processes acting on orbitals \( A \) and \( B \).

The system consists of three localized orbitals \( \{A,B,C\} \), two leads \( L \) and \( R \), and a magnetic flux \( \phi \) introducing a Peierls phase.

In the localized basis:

\[ H_0 = \begin{pmatrix} \varepsilon_A & V_{AB} & V_{AC} \\ V_{AB} & \varepsilon_B & V_{BC} e^{i\alpha} \\ V_{AC} & V_{BC} e^{-i\alpha} & \varepsilon_C \end{pmatrix}. \]

The phase \( \alpha \) represents the Aharonov-Bohm magnetic flux.

The lead self-energies are:

\[ \Sigma_L = - i \Gamma_L \ket{A}\bra{A}, \qquad \Sigma_R = - i \Gamma_R \ket{B}\bra{B}. \]

Each orbital interacts independently with an environment. This is modeled by imaginary self-energies:

\[ \Sigma_A^{(\phi)} = - i \Gamma_{\phi A}\ket{A}\bra{A}, \qquad \Sigma_B^{(\phi)} = - i \Gamma_{\phi B}\ket{B}\bra{B}. \]

The effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian becomes:

\[ H_{\text{eff}} = H_0 + \sum_\alpha \Sigma_\alpha \]
And the Green's function is:

(in its matrix form)

\[ G^R(E) = \left(\begin{matrix} a & -V_{AB} & -V_{AC} \\ -V_{AB} & b & -V_{BC}e^{i\alpha}\\ -V_{AC} & -V_{BC}e^{-i\alpha} & c \\ \end{matrix} \right)^{-1} \]

where we have taken

\[ \begin{align*} a &= E - \varepsilon_A - \Sigma_A - \Sigma_A^{(\phi)} \\ b &= E - \varepsilon_B - \Sigma_B - \Sigma_B^{(\phi)} \\ c &= E - \varepsilon_C \end{align*} \]

Or more compactly:

\[ G^R(E) = \left[ E I - H_{\text{eff}} \right]^{-1}. \]

Using the Fisher-Lee relation in matrix form:

\[ T_{LR}(E) = \mathrm{Tr} \left[ \mathbf{\Gamma}_L G^R \mathbf{\Gamma}_R G^A \right], \]

where

\[ \mathbf{\Gamma}_L = 2\Gamma_L \ket{A}\bra{A}, \qquad \mathbf{\Gamma}_R = 2\Gamma_R \ket{B}\bra{B}. \]

Subsituting these in the Fisher-Lee relation we get:

\[ T_{LR}(E) = 4\Gamma_L\Gamma_R \left| G_{AB}^R(E) \right|^2 \]
Using the cofactor formula: \[ G_{AB}^R(E) = \frac{\left| \begin{matrix} -V_{AB} & -V_{AC} \\ -V_{BC}e^{-i\alpha} & c \end{matrix} \right| }{Det\left[ E I - H_{\text{eff}} \right]} \]

And calculating the determinant in the numerator it results in:

\[ G_{AB}^R(E) = \frac{V_{AB}(E-\varepsilon_C) + V_{AC}V_{BC}e^{-i\alpha}}{\text{Det}\left[ E I - H_{\text{eff}} \right]} \]

From which it follows:

\[ T_{LR}(E,\alpha) = 4\Gamma_L\Gamma_R \frac{\left|V_{AB}(E-\varepsilon_C) + V_{AC}V_{BC}e^{-i\alpha} \right|^2}{ \left| \text{Det}\left[ E I - H_{\text{eff}}\right] \right|^2} \]
From this last expresion, the antiresonance condition can be seen to be: \[ \begin{align*} 0 &= V_{AB}(E-\varepsilon_C) + V_{AC}V_{BC}e^{-i\alpha} \\ E - \varepsilon_C &= -\frac{V_{AC}V_{BC}e^{-i\alpha}}{V_{AB}} \\ E &= \varepsilon_C -\frac{V_{AC}V_{BC}e^{-i\alpha}}{V_{AB}} \end{align*} \]

Then, the real part is:

\[ E_{\text{anti}} = \varepsilon_C -\frac{V_{AC}V_{BC}}{V_{AB}}cos(\alpha) \]

It is very interesting to note that \( \Gamma_\phi \) does not appear in this last expression. In other words, the antiresoncance is not shifted by decoherence, thought it may be lifted. This can be seen from the denominator:

\[ \left| \text{Det}\left[ E I - H_{\text{eff}}\right] \right|^2 = \left| a b c - a V_{BC}^2 - b V_{AC}^2 - c V_{AB}^2 + 2 V_{AB} V_{AC} V_{BC} \cos\alpha \right|^2 \]

where all decoherence rates \( \Gamma_{\phi A}, \Gamma_{\phi B} \) enter exclusively through the complex quantities \(a\) and \(b\). Therefore, decoherence modifies only the pole structure of the Green function.

Resonances and Broadening

Resonances occur when

\[ \text{Det}\left[ E I - H_{\text{eff}}\right] = 0. \]

Because \(a\) and \(b\) contain imaginary contributions, the poles acquire an imaginary part:

\[ E_n \rightarrow E_n - i\Gamma_{\text{eff}}. \]

Thus, the effective width of each resonance becomes

\[ \Gamma_{\text{eff}} \sim \Gamma + \Gamma_\phi. \]

This implies:

  • Resonance peaks broaden with decoherence.
  • Peak heights decrease.
  • Energy positions remain essentially unchanged.

Lifting of the Antiresonance

The transmission zero is determined solely by the numerator:

\[ V_{AB}(E-\varepsilon_C) + V_{AC}V_{BC}e^{-i\alpha}. \]

Decoherence does not modify this interference condition. However, since the denominator now contains finite imaginary parts, the exact cancellation of amplitudes becomes ineffective.

At the antiresonance energy:

\[ E = E_{\text{anti}}, \]

the transmission becomes

\[ T_{\min} \sim \frac{\Gamma_\phi^2}{|\text{Det}\left[ E_\text{anti} I - H_{\text{eff}}\right]|^2}. \]

Therefore:

  • \( \Gamma_\phi = 0 \) → perfect destructive interference.
  • Small \( \Gamma_\phi \) → quadratic lifting of the zero.
  • Large \( \Gamma_\phi \) → interference progressively suppressed.

Strong Decoherence Limit

For

\[ \Gamma_\phi \gg V_{ij}, \]

the determinant is dominated by imaginary terms:

\[ \text{Det}\left[ E_\text{anti} I - H_{\text{eff}}\right] \approx (E+i\Gamma_\phi)^2(E-\varepsilon_C). \]

In this limit:

  • The flux dependence disappears.
  • Aharonov-Bohm oscillations vanish.
  • Transport becomes sequential and classical.

Physical Interpretation

In coherent transport, amplitudes interfere:

\[ |A_1 + A_2|^2. \]

Decoherence converts this into an incoherent sum of probabilities:

\[ |A_1|^2 + |A_2|^2. \]

This is the essence of the D'Amato-Pastawski model: imaginary self-energies introduce irreversible phase randomization while preserving current conservation through the voltage-probe condition.

Consequently:

  • Antiresonances are lifted but not shifted.
  • Resonances broaden.
  • Aharonov-Bohm oscillations decay continuously with increasing decoherence.
