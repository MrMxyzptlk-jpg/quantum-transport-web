Problem 2 - Resonant Tunneling with Decoherence (DP Model)

Interactive tight-binding model for resonant tunneling with decoherence (DP Model)

Problem Assignment

Solve the transport problem through a resonant state in the presence of a decoherent process described by an effective self-energy:

\[ \Sigma_\phi^R = - i \Gamma_\phi \]

This corresponds to the D'Amato-Pastawski effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian model.

  1. Solve in detail the Landauer-Büttiker current conservation equations for this model.
  2. Evaluate analytically and numerically the effective transmission \( T_{LR}\).
  3. Plot and discuss the behavior as a function of the incident Fermi energy.

References:

  • D'Amato-Pastawski (PRB 1990)
  • Pastawski-Medina (2001)
  • Pastawski, Foa-Torres, Medina (2002)
Theoretical Derivation

1. Solving the Landauer-Büttiker current conservation equations

We consider a single resonant level of energy \( \varepsilon_0 \) coupled to two leads \(L, R\) and to a fictitious probe \( \phi \) that models decoherence.

The retarded self-energies are taken in the wide-band limit:

\[ \Sigma_\alpha^R = - i \Gamma_\alpha, \qquad \alpha = L, R, \phi. \]

The effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian becomes:

\[ \begin{align*} H_{\text{eff}} &= \varepsilon_0 + \left( \Sigma_L + \Sigma_R + \Sigma_\phi \right) \\ &= \varepsilon_0 - i \left( \Gamma_L + \Gamma_R + \Gamma_\phi \right). \end{align*} \]

The retarded Green function for this single site-system is:

\[ G^R(E) = \frac{1} {E - \varepsilon_0 + i \Gamma_{\text{tot}}}, \]

where

\[ \Gamma_{\text{tot}} = \Gamma_L + \Gamma_R + \Gamma_\phi. \]

Its modulus squared is:

\[ |G^R(E)|^2 = \frac{1} {(E - \varepsilon_0)^2 + \Gamma_{\text{tot}}^2}. \]

For three terminals, the current at terminal \( \alpha \) is:

\[ I_\alpha = \frac{2e}{h} \sum_\beta T_{\alpha\beta} (\mu_\alpha - \mu_\beta). \]

Using the Fisher-Lee relation:

\[ T_{\alpha\beta}(E) = 4 \Gamma_\alpha \Gamma_\beta |G^R(E)|^2. \]

Therefore,

\[ T_{\alpha\beta}(E) = \frac{ 4 \Gamma_\alpha \Gamma_\beta} { (E - \varepsilon_0)^2 + \Gamma_{\text{tot}}^2 }. \]

The fictitious probe does not inject net current:

\[ I_\phi = 0. \]

From the Landauer-Büttiker equation:

\[ 0 = T_{\phi L} (\mu_\phi - \mu_L) + T_{\phi R} (\mu_\phi - \mu_R). \]

Solving for \( \mu_\phi \):

\[ \mu_\phi = \frac{ T_{\phi L} \mu_L + T_{\phi R} \mu_R }{ T_{\phi L} + T_{\phi R} }. \]
Substituting back into the current expression for \(I_L\), one obtains the effective two-terminal transmission: \[ \begin{align*} I_L &= \frac{2e}{h} \sum_\beta T_{L\beta}(\mu_L - \mu_\beta) \\ &= \frac{2e}{h} \left[ T_{LR}(\mu_L - \mu_R)+ T_{L\phi}(\mu_L - \mu_\phi) \right] \\ &= \frac{2e}{h} \left[ T_{LR}(\mu_L - \mu_R)+ T_{L\phi}\left(\mu_L - \frac{T_{\phi L} \mu_L + T_{\phi R} \mu_R }{T_{\phi L} + T_{\phi R}} \right) \right] \\ &= \frac{2e}{h} \left[ T_{LR}(\mu_L - \mu_R) - T_{L\phi}\left( \frac{T_{\phi R} \mu_L - T_{\phi R} \mu_R }{T_{\phi L} + T_{\phi R}} \right) \right] \\ &= \frac{2e}{h} \left[ T_{LR}(\mu_L - \mu_R) - T_{L\phi}T_{R\phi}\left( \frac{\mu_L - \mu_R}{T_{\phi L} + T_{\phi R}} \right) \right] \\ &= \frac{2e}{h} \left[ T_{LR} - \left( \frac{T_{L\phi}T_{R\phi}}{T_{\phi L} + T_{\phi R}} \right) \right](\mu_L + \mu_R) \\ &= \frac{2e}{h} \left[ T_{LR} + \frac{T_{L\phi}T_{\phi R}}{T_{\phi L} + T_{\phi R}} \right](\mu_L + \mu_R) \\ &= \frac{2e}{h} T_{LR}^{\text{eff}} (\mu_L + \mu_R) \\ \end{align*} \]

Where we have identified the effective transmision:

\[ T_{LR}^{\text{eff}} = T_{LR} + \frac{T_{L\phi} T_{\phi R}}{T_{L\phi} + T_{\phi R}}. \]
Finally, replacing \( T_{\alpha \beta} \) in the last expression: \[ \begin{align*} T_{LR}^{\text{eff}} &= T_{LR} + \frac{T_{L\phi} T_{\phi R}}{T_{L\phi} + T_{\phi R}} \\ &= \frac{4}{ (E - \varepsilon_0)^2 + \Gamma_{\text{tot}}^2 } \left( \Gamma_L\Gamma_R + \frac{\Gamma_L\Gamma_\phi \Gamma_\phi \Gamma_R }{\Gamma_L\Gamma_\phi + \Gamma_\phi \Gamma_R } \right). \\ \end{align*} \]

Simplifying, we get:

\[ T_{LR}^{\text{eff}}(E) = \frac{4 \Gamma_L \Gamma_R}{(E - \varepsilon_0)^2 + \Gamma_{\text{tot}}^2} \left(1 + \frac{\Gamma_\phi}{\Gamma_L + \Gamma_R} \right). \]

Physical Limits

  • \( \Gamma_\phi = 0 \): fully coherent resonance.
  • Small \( \Gamma_\phi \): partial decoherence and resonance broadening.
  • \( \Gamma_\phi \to \infty \): transport becomes classical.

This model describes the crossover from coherent quantum tunneling to incoherent transport induced by environmental interactions.
