Problem 1 - Double Quantum Dot & Aharonov-Bohm Effect

Interactive tight-binding model for a Double Quantum Dot (Landauer formalism)

Problem Assignment

The Double Quantum Dot (DQD) consists of two consecutive Double-Barrier Resonant Tunneling Diodes (DBRTD). The system is schematically represented by two localized orbitals \( A \) and \( B \) (for \( V_U = 0 \)).

  1. Derive analytical expressions for the Landauer conductance using the Fisher-Lee relation.
  2. Plot the transmission function for several parameter choices and discuss the physical behavior.
  3. Add a third orbital \( C \) \((V_U \neq 0)\) providing an additional tunneling channel. Introduce a magnetic field producing a flux represented by a phase \( 0 \leq \phi \leq 2\pi \) (singular gauge).
  4. Discuss interference effects (Aharonov-Bohm effect).
  5. Identify antiresonances and compare them with the antiresonance phenomenon in two coupled pendulums.

Reference: D'Amato, Pastawski, Weisz (PRB 1989)

Theoretical Derivation

1. From the Fisher-Lee relation to the Landauer conductance

We consider two single-level quantum dots:

  • Orbital \(A\)
  • Orbital \(B\)
  • Coupling: \( V_{AB}\)

The left (right) lead couples to \(A\,(B)\) via \( V_L\,(V_R) \).

The Hamiltonian is then:

\[ H = \left(\begin{matrix} \varepsilon_A & V_{AB} \\ V_{AB}^* & \varepsilon_B \end{matrix} \right) \]
Then, the Green's function is: \[ \begin{align} G &= \left[ \varepsilon I - H - \Sigma \right] ^{-1} \\ &= \left(\begin{matrix} \varepsilon - \varepsilon_A - \Sigma_L & -V_{AB} \\ -V_{AB}^* & \varepsilon - \varepsilon_B - \Sigma_R \end{matrix} \right)^{-1} \end{align} \] where \( \Sigma = \Sigma_L\ket{A}\bra{A} - \Sigma_R\ket{B}\bra{B} \) is the coupling to the leads at A (left lead) and B (right lead) respectively.

Taking \( a = \varepsilon - \varepsilon_A - \Sigma_L \) and \( b = \varepsilon - \varepsilon_B - \Sigma_R \) the Green's function takes the form:

\[ G = \left(\begin{matrix} a & -V_{AB} \\ -V_{AB}^* & b \end{matrix} \right)^{-1} \]

Which can trivially be inverted to get:

\[ G = \frac{1}{D} \left(\begin{matrix} b & -V_{AB} \\ -V_{AB}^* & a \end{matrix} \right) \]

where D is the determinant \( D = \text{Det}\{ \varepsilon I - H - \Sigma \} = ab - |V_{AB}|^2\)

Remembering the Fisher-Lee relation,

\[ T_{L,R}(E) = \text{Tr} \left\{ 2\Gamma_R G^R 2\Gamma_L G^A \right\} \]

and using the fact that the \( \Gamma \) matrices are diagonal in this case, we have:

\[ T_{L,R}(E) = 4 \Gamma_R \Gamma_L \left| G_{AB}^R \right|^2 \]
Substituting this result in the Landauer formula for the conductance \( G_{LR} \) (at \( T = 0K \)) we get: \[ \begin{align} G_{L,R} &= \frac{2e^2}{h} T_{L,R}(E_F) \\ &= \frac{2e^2}{h} \text{Tr} \left\{ 2\Gamma_R G_{AB}^R 2\Gamma_L G_{AB}^A \right\} \\ &= \frac{8e^2}{h} \Gamma_R \Gamma_L \left| G_{AB}^R \right|^2 \\ \end{align} \]

Using the explicit expression for the Green's function, we may identify the element \( G_{AB}^R = -\frac{V_{AB}}{D}. \) The resulting conductance, then, is:

\[ G_{L,R} = \frac{8e^2}{h} \Gamma_R \Gamma_L \left| \frac{V_{AB}}{D} \right|^2 \]

Finally, substituting \(D\), the full expresion is:

\[ G_{L,R} = \frac{8e^2}{h} \Gamma_R \Gamma_L \frac{|V_{AB}|^2}{\left| (\varepsilon - \varepsilon_A - \Sigma_L)(\varepsilon - \varepsilon_B - \Sigma_R) - |V_{AB}|^2 \right|^2} \]
